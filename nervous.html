Which photoreceptor cells are responsible for color vision and help create very sharp images?
   
   
   
   
Answer:

Cones are the photoreceptor cells responsible for color vision and sharp images. Rods detect lightning, while rhodopsin is a photosensitive molecule within rods that respond to light.

Psychotropic medications can cause destabilization of thermoregulation due to:
   
   
   
   
Answer:

Primary mental health chemical imbalances or treatment with psychotropic medication can alter calcium levels of the brain, which stimulate the hypothalamus and thus thermoregulation.


Sleep Disturbances
Which of the following waveforms is most commonly found with light sleepers?
   
   
   
   
Answer:

Beta waves are typically found in awake, conscious individuals. Alpha waves are typically found in light, NREM sleep. Theta waves are found in Stage 2 NREM sleep, along with sleep spindles and K-complexes. Delta waves are found in stage 3 and 4 NREM.

Nervous System Trauma
Disorders of the nervous system resulting from a tumor in a different area of the body are known as:
   
   
   
   
Answer:

Some tumors cause an autoantibody immune response. These antibodies often not only attack the tumor, but also attack a portion of the nervous system not related to the tumor. These conditions are known as paraneoplastic syndrome and may be distant or apparently unrelated to the tumor site.

Which autoimmune condition is characterized by the body producing autoantibodies that attack muscle receptor sites, reducing the ability to receive neurotransmitter signals to move properly, thus causing weakness?
   
   
   
   
Answer:

MG is caused by the body producing autoantibodies against ACh receptors, causing inappropriate lack of response to stimulation. Muscular dystrophy is caused by a genetic deficiency of dystrophin, a major muscle component. Muscular degenerative disorder is a symptom-based category.


Physical Exam Findings Related to the Nervous System and Mental Health
You are caring for an adult patient who is suspected to have upper neuronal damage. Which provocation do you perform?
   
   
   
   
Answer:

A patient who has upper neuronal damage will demonstrate a positive Babinski sign, where the big toe extends and the rest of the toes fan out when the sole is stroked. A negative Babinski sign, normal in patients above 2 years of age, causes all toes to curl inward.
A patient with cerebellar dysfunction may exhibit dysmetria, a lack of coordination, in the finger-to-nose test. Romberg's test is positive when a standing patient demonstrates imbalance when the eyes are closed and demonstrates sensory ataxia.
The Jendrassik maneuver, where the patient clenches their teeth, and interlocks flexed fingers, is used in combination with patellar reflex testing to elicit a stronger reaction from muscle tension increasing afferent activity.

Answer:
