A 32 yo M presents s/p MVA with a laceration on the medial right knee complaining of numbness along the medial right leg and foot, no motor deficit. Which nerve was injured?






Answer:

The saphenous nerve, which travels with the great saphenous vein, runs along the medial leg and thigh to provide cutaneous innervation. The femoral nerve innervates the anterior thigh, the sural nerve runs up the lateral leg with the lesser saphenous vein to innervate the posterior lower leg and lateral foot. The superficial fibular nerve innervates the anterior leg muscle and the skin between the 1st and 2nd toes- lesions would lead to foot drop.

A patient presents with a diabetic ulcer in the anterior midline of the ankle region experienced loss of cutaneous sensation on the dorsal foot, likely due to damage from:






Answer:

The fibular nerve branches into the superficial fibular nerve, which runs anteriorly to the ankle and innervates the lower anterior leg and dorsum of the foot, and the lateral sural cutaneous nerve, which innervates the lateral leg. The femoral nerve innervates the quadriceps femoris, sartorius, and pectineus and gives off the anterior femoral cutaneous branch to the medial and anterior thigh skin, and the saphenous nerve, which innervates the skin of the medial leg and foot. The sural nerve on the posterior leg adjacent to the lesser saphenous vein innervates the skin of the posterior lower leg and lateral foot.

A cashier develops painful and tortuous varicose veins in her lower legs due to prolonged standing, leading to failure of the valves of the:






Answer:

The perforating veins shunt blood from the superficial to deep veins and are the first to suffer valve failures due to prolonged standing; the blood subsequently backs up in the superficial veins. The larger veins do not visibly varicose.
