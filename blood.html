Cardiovascular Chest Pain
A patient tells you he has paroxysmic atrial fibrillation. Which symptoms would you expect him to report?
   
   
   
   
Answer:

A - In medicine, when a condition is noted as paroxysmal, it means that the condition is not constant and that it occurs occasionally with symptoms that come and go and tend to not last that long. The symptoms and condition may resolve themselves without medical intervention. Medical intervention can shorten the symptoms and/or decrease the amount of times the symptoms occur.


When a patient has inadequate blood supply to the heart, what is the resulting chest pain called?
   
   
   
   
Answer:

Angina is the chest pain resulting from insufficient blood supply to the heart and is treated by vasodilators such as nitroglycerin.

Which of the following segments of an EKG can remain abnormal weeks to months after an acute myocardial infarction?




Answer:

A - In an acute myocardial infarction, the EKG changes over time. The initial changes are shown in ST segment elevation, which decreases over time to appear normal again. Weeks to months after an acute myocardial infarction, the Q wave can stay decreased. In the same time frame, the P and T waves tend to go back to an almost near normal quality.
